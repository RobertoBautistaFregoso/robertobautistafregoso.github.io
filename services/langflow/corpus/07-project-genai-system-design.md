# Project — GenAI System Design

Roberto is deepening production GenAI system design: the architecture behind retrieval,
evaluation, agentic systems, and the cost/latency tradeoffs of shipping LLM products.

**The problem.** AI product managers who can't reason about the system under the product
end up negotiating roadmaps they don't understand — retrieval quality, evaluation, latency
and cost budgets, agent architectures. Roberto wanted to hold his own in those design
conversations, not just relay them.

**What he's doing.** He is working through a structured GenAI system design program (Maven),
going deep on the architecture decisions behind production LLM products: retrieval-augmented
generation (RAG) and when it beats fine-tuning, evaluation design for non-deterministic
systems, agentic patterns and tool use, and the cost/latency tradeoffs that decide what
actually ships. Alongside the coursework he pressure-tests the concepts in practice
projects — including the agentic workflow that builds his portfolio site.

**Where it's landing.** In progress. The immediate payoff shows up in client work: sharper
questions in architecture reviews, more realistic eval-first scoping, and AI product
decisions argued from how the system actually behaves rather than from vendor decks.

Skills exercised: GenAI, system design, RAG, evals.
