# Success Definition: Household Spending Visibility (v0)

**Date:** 2026-05-20
**Related:** `02-problem-statement.md`, `01-problem-framing.md`

---

## The definition

> **This app is a success if, 2 weeks after shipping, I'm checking it weekly without forcing myself, each import-and-review session stays ≤ 10 minutes, and the categories are stable enough month-to-month that I'd make a real spending decision off the comparison.**

---

## The three measurable checks (verifiable in 2 weeks)

1. **Behavior** — did I open it weekly, unprompted? *(The real change: rarely → weekly. This is the behavior-change bet, and the most likely way the app fails — if I'm not opening it weekly, friction wasn't reduced enough.)*
2. **Cost** — did each session stay ≤ 10 minutes? *(Low enough that the weekly habit survives.)*
3. **Trust** — did I make, or would I confidently make, a spending decision off the month-over-month view? *(Stability is the bar — my core differentiator.)*

---

## Deliberately excluded (and why)

- **"Save X hours/week"** — excluded. Current spend is ~10 min/week; there's almost no time to claw back. The value is behavior + visibility, not time saved. Measuring time-saved would make a successful app look like a failure.
- **"Make better financial decisions"** — excluded. No bad-decision problem exists (no concrete bad outcome ever occurred). The validated pain is feeling *lost*, not making *mistakes*.

---

## For Phase 8 (Iterate & Measure)

These three checks ARE the Phase 8 metrics-review inputs. Do not let the success metric drift toward financial-outcome or time-saved framing — measure behavior (weekly use), cost (≤10 min), and trust (decision-grade stability).
