**The problem.** AI product managers who can't reason about the system under the product end up negotiating roadmaps they don't understand — retrieval quality, evaluation, latency and cost budgets, agent architectures. I wanted to be able to hold my own in those design conversations, not just relay them.

**What I'm doing.** I'm working through a structured GenAI system design program (Maven), going deep on the architecture decisions behind production LLM products: retrieval-augmented generation and when it beats fine-tuning, evaluation design for non-deterministic systems, agentic patterns and tool use, and the cost/latency tradeoffs that decide what actually ships. Alongside the coursework I pressure-test the concepts in practice projects — including the agentic workflow that builds this site.

**Where it's landing.** In progress. The immediate payoff shows up in client work: sharper questions in architecture reviews, more realistic eval-first scoping, and AI product decisions argued from how the system actually behaves rather than from vendor decks.
